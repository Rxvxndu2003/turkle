Introduction

A turtle hatchery is a conservation project aimed at protecting sea turtle species and their habitats. In Sri Lanka, these hatcheries have become popular tourist attractions due to the importance of sea turtles to the local marine ecosystem and the unique opportunity to observe the hatching and release of baby turtles into the ocean.Visitors to these hatcheries can witness firsthand the careful collection and incubation of turtle eggs, the hatching of baby turtles, and the release of these hatchlings back into the sea. The experience provides a unique opportunity to learn about these fascinating creatures and the vital role they play in maintaining healthy marine ecosystems.

Turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka not only contribute to the preservation of sea turtle populations but also provide sustainable livelihoods for local communities through tourism. These projects serve as excellent examples of how conservation efforts can benefit both wildlife and people, and visitors to Sri Lanka should consider a visit to a turtle hatchery as a must-do activity.

There are seven different Turtle species in the world.Among these species,five species are coming to Sri Lanka to breeding.

  1. Dermochelys criacea
  2. Caretta caretta
  3. Lepidochelys olivacea
  4. Eretmochelys imbricata
  5. Chelonia mydas

Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a conservation center located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles, which are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and other human activities. The center's primary objective is to promote the conservation of these gentle creatures by protecting their eggs, incubating them in a safe environment, and releasing the hatchlings back into the ocean. Additionally, the center provides educational programs and information to the public on the importance of turtle conservation and the role that everyone can play in helping to protect these beautiful creatures. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular tourist attraction in Sri Lanka and an excellent destination for anyone interested in learning more about the conservation of sea turtles.

Kosgoda

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a conservation center located in the coastal town of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles, which are threatened by a variety of factors including habitat loss, poaching, and pollution. The primary objective of the center is to promote the conservation of these magnificent creatures by safeguarding their eggs, incubating them in a controlled environment, and releasing the hatchlings back into the ocean. In addition to this, the center offers educational programs and information to the public on the importance of sea turtle conservation and the role that everyone can play in helping to protect these amazing creatures. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a popular tourist attraction in Sri Lanka and an excellent opportunity for visitors to learn about sea turtle conservation and observe these incredible creatures up close.

Bentota

Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a conservation center located in the coastal town of Bentota, Sri Lanka. The hatchery is committed to preserving and protecting sea turtles, which are in danger due to a variety of factors such as poaching, habitat loss, and pollution. The hatchery's primary goal is to help increase the population of these endangered species by safeguarding their eggs, hatching them in a controlled environment, and releasing the hatchlings back into the sea. The center also offers educational programs and information to the public on the importance of sea turtle conservation and the role that everyone can play in helping to safeguard these magnificent creatures. The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a popular tourist destination in Sri Lanka and an excellent opportunity for visitors to learn about sea turtle conservation and observe these magnificent creatures up close.
